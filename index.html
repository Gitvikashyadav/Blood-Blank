Types of Donation

The average human body contains about five litres of blood, which is made of several cellular and non-cellular components such as Red blood cell, Platelet, and Plasma.

What is it?

Blood collected straight from the donor into a blood bag and mixed with an anticoagulant is called whole blood. It is then centrifuged to separate red cells, which are mixed with a preservative to be called packed red blood cells.
